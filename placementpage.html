PLACEMENT POLICY

Charotar University of Science and Technology (CHARUSAT) believes in producing graduates with impactful employability skills. Towards this, a dedicated University level Career Development and Placement Cell (CDPC) has been constituted for serving students of all the programmes of Charusat. Out of several activities of CDPC, one activity is to help and facilitate students to obtain job placements. This policy lays down rules for this activity.


Eligibility For Campus Placement

Following are the eligibility criteria for placement drives :

  • A Student must exhibit strong intentions for Placement. Any Student who wants to go for Higher Studies or wants to become an entrepreneur shall not appear for the Placements.

  • Students with attendance record of minimum 80% and above will be eligible for the campus placement assistance.

  • Attendance for the Pre-Placement Talk is compulsory for the Placement aspiring students.

Preparation for Placement

CDPC conducts various activities to make student employable like Aptitude Preparation, Technical and soft skill expert sessions, domain knowledge sessions by industry experts and mock interviews. Placement desirous students must attended all the sessions organized by CDPC.


Placement Drive

CDPC approaches different organization for Campus Placement Drive for students. This can be On-campus at CHARUSAT campus or Off-campus (students need to visit company premises or other location for selection process). CDPC guarantees assistance to each eligible student at each stage of selection process to achieve their desirous job. However, placements are dependent on economic situation and student’s performance.

Furthermore, the following need to be noted regarding On-campus and Off-campus processes :

a) On-campus placement - companies visiting campus (in-person or virtually) - All drives will be considered as on-campus wherever the data of students has been submitted via CDPC team (centrally or at department/institute level) - Students may also appear directly (in- person or virtually) as per the decisions made by companies in which case, they will be considered in on-campus placement category. Any student who directly applies to the companies visiting CHARUSAT by any means will be considered under on-campus activities and will have to follow the placement policy. CDPC will help students at each level up to the joining stage (in case of I+J) and also through the policy decisions required to be taken at the companies in the future.

b) Off-campus - student(s) who are applying to the companies not associated with CHARUSAT without any help from CHARUSAT, will be counted as off-campus applicants. In this case, CDPC may only support the students for the interview preparations and will not be responsible for the decisions made by the companies.


Selection Process

The selection process may comprise of all or any of the combination of the following :

1) Aptitude Test (written or online)
2) Technical Test (written or online)
3) Group Discussion
4) HR Round (Personal Interview)
5) Technical Round (Personal Interview)

A student is expected to perform excellently in each of the above stages to assure a placement. He/she should start his/her preparation from day one he/she takes admission.


Post Placement

Every company visiting on/off campus declares their result on a same day after completion of selection process. CDPC immediately declares the result to students once it is received from company. The University follows the policy of ‘one student one job’. Student will be considered as hired if his/her name appears in the list of selected candidates by any one company. No student will be allowed to appear for another job if placed in one company. In case a student gets selection from more than two companies on same day then the student can select any company based upon his/her preference.


General Rules and Regulations

1) Do’s :

  • All the students appearing in campus recruitment process are required to come in formal dressing and formal shoes.

  • Students must carry their I-cards all times during interviews and screening tests.

  • Students should report 15 Minutes prior to reporting time and be present during the attendance taken for the day.

  • All students must carry two copies of updated resume recommended by CDPC. He/she should also carry other documents such as photographs, mark sheets, etc. as informed for any particular Campus drive.

  • Students must keep themselves well informed by referring to their email communications or WhatsApp group or Facebook group or CDPC for information about placement opportunities.

  • For any queries, students should contact their Departmental Career Development and Placement representative faculty or student.

2) Don’ts :

  • Indiscipline or Impersonation in tests or any kind of malpractices during the entire Campus Placement Process will be treated as a serious offence.

  • If the student is not appearing any campus drives after registration without informing CDPC team, he/she will be considered placed and will be allowed to sit in any other campus drives only after the remaining students are placed.

  • If a student has applied in the organization / opening, he / she must complete the entire evaluation process set by the company. This includes attending pre-placement talk until the final round of interview.

  • After getting selected, if the student does not accept the placement offer, Institute/University shall take strict actions.

  • Selected/Placed students who apply to the next company without taking consent from the CDPC team will be considered out of the placement process and will not be eligible to get support from CDPC for any matters concerning with the selected company or for any future work.

  • Students can not reject the company offer (even after an internship) in any case. If they want to do so, they will need to discuss it with the CDPC team immediately. They may reject the offer only after the formal confirmation from CDPC. Otherwise, they will be subjected to the strict actions as described in the placement policy.

  • Selected student has to work with that organization for stipulated time. Student will be considered as placed. He/ She can’t leave that organization without “No Objection Certificate” from that organization.

  • Any student found of such act will be referred to the university authorities for disciplinary action which may include withholding Degree Certificate /Character Certificate / NOC / Recommendation Letter/ Reference Letter etc. from the University.

  • A list of companies coming for on-campus recruitment will be put up on CDPC website. Students must NOT apply to any of these companies off-campus through email or phone without consent of CDPC.

  • Students are not allowed to appear in final selection process (test/GD/interview etc) of companies they have not applied to through CDPC or have not been shortlisted in those companies. Students must refrain from contacting personnel of such companies when they are on-campus. Violation of this will attract deregistration and suitable disciplinary action.

CDPC will put all the efforts for placement of the students. However, it does not guarantee the placement to every desirous student as it is dependent on company policy and student’s performance.

(All decisions regarding Placements taken by CDPC will be Final and binding to all.)
